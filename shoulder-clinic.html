Shoulder Clinic

The shoulder is one of the most mobile joints in your body, making it more prone to injuries and chronic pain. Whether you're dealing with stiffness, inflammation, or trauma-related damage, our Shoulder Clinic is equipped to help restore your shoulder strength and flexibility.

Common Types of Shoulder Pain

Frozen Shoulder – Also known as adhesive capsulitis, it causes stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint. It's common in people who’ve kept their shoulder immobilized for a long time.

Rotator Cuff Injuries – The rotator cuff is a group of muscles and tendons that keep your shoulder stable. Tears or inflammation can result in persistent pain, especially at night or while lifting your arm.

Shoulder Impingement – This occurs when shoulder blade rubs against tendons, causing pain when raising arms above the head. Common among athletes and those with repetitive overhead activity.

Dislocation – When the upper arm bone pops out of the shoulder socket, causing intense pain, swelling, and immobility.

Arthritis – Osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis can affect the shoulder, leading to stiffness, swelling, and pain during movement.
